Elon Musk, CEO of Tesla and SpaceX

    A Timeline of Elon Musk's life

  • 1971 - Born June 28th in Pretoria, Transvaal, South Africa
  • 1980 - Parent's divorced. Elon chose to live with his father
  • 1982 - Being a voracious reader throughout his childhood, Elon took up computer programming and taught himself BASIC by finishing a six month course in three days.
  • 1983 - Wrote a video game named Blastar, which he sold to a magazine called PC and Office Technology for $500.
  • 1989 - Just before his 18th birthday, Elon moved to Canada after obtaining Canadian citizenship through his Canadian-born mother.
  • 1989 - At the age of 17, Elon was accepted into Queen's University in Kingston, Ontario, for undergraduate study
  • 1992 - After spending two years at Queen's University, Musk transferred to the University of Pennsylvania.
  • 1995 - In May, Elon received a Bachelor of Science degree in physics from its College of Arts and Sciences, and a Bachelor of Science degree in economics from its Wharton School of Business.
  • 1995 - Musk moved to California to begin a PhD in applied physics and materials science at Stanford University, but left the program after two days to pursue his entrepreneurial aspirations in the areas of the internet, renewable energy and outer space.
  • 1995 - Started a web software company with his brother, Kimbal, called Zip2. Marketed as an Internet city guide for the newspaper publishing industry.
  • 1999 - Compaq acquired Zip2 for $307 million in cash and $34 million in stock options in February. Elon received $22 million for his 7 percent share from the sale.
  • 1999 - In March, Musk co-founded X.com, an online financial services and e-mail payment company, with $10 million from the sale of Zip2.
  • 2000 - X.com merged with Confinity, who had a money transfer service called PayPal, and X.com and PayPal later merged in 2001, keeping the name PayPal.
  • 2002 - PayPal was acquired by eBay for $1.5 billion in stock, of which Musk received $165 million. Before its sale, Musk, who was the company's largest shareholder, owned 11.7% of PayPal's shares.
  • 2002 - Elon became a US citizen.
